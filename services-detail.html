Diabetes

Complications associated with diabetes can affect various organs and systems in the body, emphasizing the importance of proactive management and maintaining overall health.

Gestational Diabetes

Gestational diabetes is a form of diabetes that develops during pregnancy. It occurs when the hormones produced by the placenta interfere with the body's ability to use insulin effectively, resulting in high blood sugar levels.

Thyroid Disorders

Thyroid disorders refer to conditions that affect the thyroid gland, a small butterfly-shaped gland located in the neck. The thyroid gland plays a crucial role in regulating metabolism, growth, and development in the body.

All Complicated Fevers

Complex fevers, also known as complicated fevers, refer to febrile illnesses that present with additional symptoms or complications beyond a simple fever.

Hypertension

Hypertension, also known as high blood pressure, is a chronic medical condition characterized by elevated blood pressure in the arteries.

Epilepsy

It is a chronic condition that affects the brain's electrical activity, causing disruptions in normal brain function. Epileptic seizures can vary in intensity, duration, and symptoms, and they may manifest as convulsions, loss of consciousness, altered senses, or repetitive movements.

Cardiac Problems

Cardiac problems, also known as cardiovascular diseases (CVD), refer to a group of conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels.Early detection, regular check-ups, and appropriate medical intervention are crucial for managing cardiac problems.

Neurological Disorders

Neurological disorders encompass a wide range of conditions that affect the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, and nerves. These disorders can have diverse causes, symptoms, and levels of severity, and they can impact various aspects of a person's neurological functioning

Diabetic Foot

Diabetic foot refers to a range of foot complications that can occur in individuals with diabetes. By practicing good foot care habits, individuals with diabetes can significantly reduce the risk of diabetic foot complications and maintain optimal foot health.

Anaemia

Anemia is a condition characterized by a deficiency of healthy red blood cells (RBCs) or a decrease in the amount of hemoglobin in the blood.
